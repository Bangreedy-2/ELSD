\section*{2. Project Overview}

GG-Code is a domain-specific language that acts as a human-readable bridge between high-level
manufacturing intent and the low-level G-code instructions used to control CNC machines, 3D
printers, and laser engravers. Rather than writing cryptic numeric sequences, users can express
machine operations in clear, programmable syntax.

The compiler automatically translates GG-CodeSource files into:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, label={--}]
    \item Standard G-code output compatible with CNC machines and 3D printers
    \item Motion control sequences (linear movement, arc, home commands)
    \item Geometric primitive expansions (square, rectangle, circle, line)
    \item Control flow unrolling (loops, conditionals, variables)
    \item Machine configuration commands (temperature, tool change, wait)
\end{itemize}

The language grammar is formally defined using ANTLR4 and compiled through two stages:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item Lexical analysis
    \item Syntactic analysis
\end{enumerate}

The project deliverables include a domain analysis, a comparative study of existing G-code tools,
a formal grammar specification, a working parser, and a complete project report written in \LaTeX.

\section*{3. Roles and Responsibilities}

Tasks are distributed among team members based on the skills and areas identified during the
first team meeting on 29 January 2026. Each member is expected to take ownership of their work
and deliver it on time.

Areas of contribution within the team include:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, label={--}]
    \item \textbf{Grammar Design} -- defining the formal language rules and structure of GG-Code
    \item \textbf{ANTLR Implementation} -- building the lexer, parser, and AST
    \item \textbf{Testing and Debugging} -- writing test cases and verifying G-code output correctness
    \item \textbf{Documentation} -- preparing the \LaTeX\ report, meeting notes, and technical descriptions
    \item \textbf{Research and Analysis} -- collecting academic sources and performing domain analysis
\end{itemize}

When a team member faces difficulty with their task, others are expected to step in and help
rather than waiting for the problem to grow.

% ──────────────────────────────────────────

\section*{4. Communication}

The team commits to staying connected and transparent throughout the project:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, label={--}]
    \item Each member keeps the team informed about their progress, blockers, or delays.
    \item If a deadline cannot be met, the team must be notified at least two days in advance.
    \item All major decisions are made together and logged in the meeting notes.
\end{itemize}

The team communicates through the following channels:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, label={--}]
    \item Group chat on a messaging platform -- Telegram
    \item Weekly sessions with mentor Irina Cojuhari, scheduled on Fridays
    \item Video calls when in-person attendance is not possible
\end{itemize}

\section*{5. Work Expectations}

Every team member is expected to uphold the following standards:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item Deliver assigned tasks by the agreed deadline without waiting to be reminded.
    \item Submit work that is genuinely thought through, not copy-pasted or AI-generated without review.
    \item Keep teammates updated on progress at least once per week.
    \item Treat the time and effort of other members with respect.
    \item Each member must independently find and describe at least 5 research articles relevant to the project domain.
\end{enumerate}

% ──────────────────────────────────────────
%  6. CONFLICT RESOLUTION
% ──────────────────────────────────────────
\section*{6. Conflict Resolution}

Should any disagreement or tension arise within the team:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item The involved members will first try to settle the matter directly through an honest conversation.
    \item If no agreement is reached, the full team discusses and reaches a decision together.
    \item As a last resort, the situation is brought to mentor Irina Cojuhari, whose guidance will be considered final.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{7. Contribution and Accountability}

Every team member bears responsibility for the quality and completeness of their portion of
the project.

If a member consistently fails to deliver or participate, this will first be addressed within
the team. If the situation does not improve, it will be escalated to the mentor and may be
reflected in the final report.
